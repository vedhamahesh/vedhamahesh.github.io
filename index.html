Silver Award

Gold Award
Obesity is a serious issue because it increases morbidity and mortality. In India, however, this condition does not get enough attention, partly because it is a “slow-killing” disease and partly because many places, like the Sanskruti Shikar School, do not have the resources needed to take action. Since the school lacked the financial resources to completely change its operations, I created a three-pronged action plan comprising new habits that anyone could take to improve their physical and mental health: better diet, more sleep, and regular exercise.

Rubik's Cubes have always fascinated me. They have a certain simplicity and complexity to them that allow people of all ages to enjoy them. I have personally spent countless hours learning how to solve Rubik's Cubes.

Fun Fact: The 3 by 3 Rubik's Cube has 43 quintillion possible configurations in total.

One day, after trying to teach a younger relative how to solve a Rubik's Cube, I realized that it wasn’t easy teaching kids in the same way I had learned. Because their vocabulary is not yet developed, describing to them the moves and algorithms needed to solve a cube is difficult. I noticed that they responded a lot better to visual representations, so I built a computer program that simulated the process of solving a Rubik’s Cube.

However, my program was written in python, which is not compatible with the web. I have converted the program into javascript, so you can see it and interact with it right here on this website. Enjoy!
